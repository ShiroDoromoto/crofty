The easiest way to never have a site is to wait until you have the perfect plan for it. The design, the topics, the schedule, the audience. By the time it is all worked out, the moment has passed.

I started this one small. A few posts, a plain layout, no grand idea of what it would become. It can grow later, or it can stay modest. Either is fine.

What matters is that it exists, that it is mine, and that I can add to it whenever I have something to say. Starting small is still starting. The rest is just showing up again.
