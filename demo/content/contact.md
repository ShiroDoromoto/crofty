The quiet way to reach the author of a site like this is email:
[hello@example.com](mailto:hello@example.com). No form, no account, no queue — a
message lands in an inbox the way it always has.

A static site has no server to catch a form post, and that is a feature, not a
gap: there is nothing here to break into and nothing quietly collecting what you
type. When a site does want a form — a booking, a longer enquiry — the small-web
answer is to embed one from a service that handles it (Formspree, Tally, a
Google Form), so the page stays static and the form provider does the catching.
